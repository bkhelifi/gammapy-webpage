Gammapy Project Role Responsibilities

The gammapy project is made possible through the efforts of community members that perform numerous important roles. This encompasses abroad scope of responsibilities ranging from direct package development to communication, distribution, and managerial activities. In this section we list the responsibilities for each of the identified project roles. The list of people fulfilling these roles is found in the Roles section.


Coordination Committee

People working on the gammapy project are from different labs with their own involment time scale and from different countries. So a coordination of the project is needed and a unique contact point for the project is expected. The Coordination Committee (CC) aims to play this role.

Members of the CC are representatives of the main developers groups and stakeholders and are working by consensus.

The role of the CC is the overall coordination and management of the gammapy project, including:

  • Keeping a large-scale view of the Gammapy project (e.g. promotion, development strategies and priorities, publications and communications),
  • Making the link with astrophysics missions, especially CTA,
  • Taking care of funding application and manpower ressources,
  • Guarantee the project communication infrastructures (e.g. mailing list, github, ..),
  • Make decisions about Proposals for the Improvement of Gammapy (PIG).
The composition of the CC is:
  • Christoph Deil (MPIK, Germany)
  • Régis Terrier (APC, France)
  • Roberta Zanin (MPIK, Germany)
  • Bruno Khelifi (APC, France)

Release Coordinator

The role of the Release Coordinator consists on taking care of the building of a gammapy release, installing it on the distribution forge and guaranting its compatibility on different OS.


Sub-package maintainer (1 per core package sub-package)

The CC aims to assign a maintainer for each sub-package of the project.

The global role of a maintainer is to manage a sub-package of the gammapy core package, being responsible for its progress, its new features, including:

  • Evaluating and merging new pull requests,
  • Cleaning the code and taking care of its quality and the pertinance of the tests,
  • Maintaining the overall sub-package documentation.

Lead Developers

The role of the Lead Developers is complementary to the sub-package maintainers. They take care of sub-packages without maintenors, unify the coding style and the organisation of the work, teach the new developers and organize daily the development work.
